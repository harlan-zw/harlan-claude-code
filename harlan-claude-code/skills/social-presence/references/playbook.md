# Social Presence Playbook

Research-backed content strategy for growing a developer Twitter presence, based on analysis of successful accounts in the Vue/Nuxt/UnJS ecosystem.

## Benchmarks (Studied Accounts)

| Account | Followers | Key Strategy |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Anthony Fu (@antaboread) | ~50k | Daily posts, visual demos, cross-project pollination, livestreams |
| Daniel Roe (@danielroe) | ~15k | Regular Nuxt updates, community engagement, conference presence |
| Hayden Bleasel (@haydenbleasel) | ~10k | Build in public, polished visuals, ecosystem tagging, consistent cadence |
| Sebastien Chopin (@Atinux) | ~20k | Creator authority, product launches, NuxtLabs updates |

## What High-Performing Dev Tweets Have in Common

1. **Visual-first**: nearly every successful tweet includes a screenshot, GIF, or video demo
2. **Value-led opening**: first 5 words hook the reader with what they get, not what the author did
3. **Ecosystem tagging**: 1-2 relevant accounts tagged for amplification (not more)
4. **Scannable format**: short sentences, line breaks, no walls of text
5. **Authentic voice**: casual, not corporate. Typos are fine. "Excited to announce" is death.

## Content Formats That Work

### Project Launch
```
[One-line hook: what problem this solves]

[Brief description, 1-2 sentences max]

[Link or "link below"]
+ polished screenshot/demo
```

### Version Update / Changelog
```
Happy Friday! [Project] v[X.Y] just dropped:

- [Feature 1: framed as benefit]
- [Feature 2: framed as benefit]
- [Feature 3: framed as benefit]

[Link]
+ screenshot of headline feature
```

### Technical Insight
```
[Non-obvious observation or lesson learned]

[1-2 sentences of context]

[Optional: code snippet screenshot]
```

### Build-in-Public
```
[What you're working on + why]

[One interesting detail or challenge]

[Screenshot of current state, even if rough]
```

### Curated Share
```
[Your take on why this matters]

[Quote tweet or link to the thing]
```

## Hooks That Stop the Scroll

Strong openers (adapt to your voice):
- "Free tool that [does specific thing]"
- "[Surprising stat or fact about a common problem]"
- "TIL [non-obvious technical insight]"
- "Watch: [brief description of what the demo shows]"
- "[X] sites use [Y] wrong. Here's what they miss:"

Weak openers to avoid:
- "Excited to announce..."
- "After months of hard work..."
- "Thread on [topic] 🧵"
- "I just released..."
- Starting with "I" in general

## Posting Cadence

### Minimum viable consistency
- 4 tweets per week (Mon, Wed, Fri + 1 flexible)
- Never go more than 3 days without posting
- Batch-create on weekends if needed

### Timing
- Best for global dev audience: 8-10am US Eastern
- Second best: 12-2pm US Eastern
- From Australia (AEST): schedule for 11pm-1am or 3-5am

### Weekly rhythm
- **Monday**: what you're working on this week
- **Wednesday**: technical insight or tip
- **Friday**: ship something, announce it
- **Flexible**: curated share, build-in-public update, or response to trending topic

## Growth Levers

1. **Open source is distribution**: every GitHub star is a potential follower. Make repos discoverable (good README, demo screenshots).
2. **Cross-platform**: same content on Threads and LinkedIn reaches different audiences.
3. **Newsletter as hedge**: email subscribers can't be taken by algorithm changes.
4. **Reply to bigger accounts**: thoughtful replies to @antaboread, @danielroe, @Atinux get seen by their audiences.
5. **Conference talks generate follow surges**: always tweet your slides/recording.

## Anti-Patterns

- Text-only tweets (get buried by the algorithm)
- Changelog dumps (nobody reads bullet points of API changes)
- Posting once then going silent for a week
- Only posting about your own work (mix in community engagement)
- Waiting for "big enough" launches (small updates compound)
- Over-threading (single tweets outperform threads for most content)
- More than 2 account tags per tweet (looks spammy)
